Overflow refers to a condition where a container or a space exceeds its maximum capacity, often resulting in spillage, overflow errors, or other unintended consequences. This concept can apply to various contexts, from physical objects, like water overflowing from a glass, to abstract ideas, like data overflow in computer systems. In computing, for example, an overflow error occurs when a calculation exceeds the allocated memory space, leading to incorrect results or system crashes. Overflow can also metaphorically describe situations where emotions, work responsibilities, or information become overwhelming, causing stress or inefficiency. Understanding and managing overflow is essential to maintain balance and prevent negative outcomes in both everyday life and technical fields.
